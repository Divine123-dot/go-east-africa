Gishora Drum Sanctuary, Burundi

A powerful cultural site near the historic royal courts, Gishora preserves Burundi's royal drumming (ingoma) tradition. Performances are ceremonial, rhythmic and deeply symbolic; drums are woven into history, storytelling and rites. Nearby village life serves simple, hearty fare: beans, cassava, plantains and Lake Tanganyika fish. Visitors should respect protocols around ceremonies and ask before photographing performers.

Coastal Paradise: Nungwi, Zanzibar

At Unguja's northern tip, Nungwi pairs turquoise water with a living dhow building tradition. Centuries of Arab, Persian and Indian influence shape the local culture, music and spice rich cuisine: seafood curries, pilau and the famous urojo. Enjoy sunset dhow cruises, turtle projects and vibrant beach bars. Note strong tidal variations in some spots; plan swimming and snorkeling around high tide.
